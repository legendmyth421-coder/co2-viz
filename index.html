About This Visualization

Question

Which countries drove global CO2 emissions growth from 1990 to 2019? This visualization allows users to interactively explore absolute CO2 emission trajectories for five major economies — the United States, China, Germany, India, and Japan — and investigate how their roles in global emissions have shifted over three decades.

Design Rationale

A multi-line time-series chart was chosen because it is the most natural encoding for continuous change over time across multiple entities. Color encodes country identity using a consistent, distinct palette, enabling easy comparison. Absolute emissions (kilotons) rather than per-capita figures were used because the question concerns total climate impact. Alternatives considered included a stacked area chart (rejected — harder to read individual trajectories) and a bar chart race (rejected — obscures long-term trend shapes).

Four interaction techniques were implemented: (1) country toggle checkboxes allow users to focus on specific countries without visual clutter; (2) a dual year-range slider supports temporal zooming to examine sub-periods closely; (3) hover highlighting with line bolding and dimming helps isolate a single country's trajectory; (4) an interactive tooltip provides precise values on demand. Together these support the task of identifying and comparing emissions drivers across the full period as well as within chosen sub-periods.

Data Source

World Bank Open Data — CO2 emissions (kt): https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/EN.ATM.CO2E.KT

Development Process

This project was completed individually. Total development time was approximately 6 hours. The most time-consuming aspect was implementing the synchronized dual-handle year-range slider in vanilla JavaScript/D3, and ensuring smooth animated axis and line transitions when filters change. Data preparation and the write-up each took approximately one hour.
