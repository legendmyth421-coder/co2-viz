About This Visualization

Question

Which countries drove global CO2 emissions growth from 1990 to 2023, and how do their trajectories look in both absolute and per-capita terms? This dashboard lets users interactively explore emission trends for 11 major economies across multiple coordinated views.

Design Rationale

The main view uses a multi-line time-series chart with gradient fills beneath each line, reinforcing country identity and making the area of change visually apparent. A stacked area mode toggle reveals cumulative totals and relative share dynamics. A coordinated horizontal bar chart shows ranked emissions for a draggable "scrubber" year, with flag icons and rank positions that animate smoothly frame by frame. A donut chart shows each country's share of the active total for the selected year. A full-width choropleth world map colors each country on a yellow-to-red sequential scale, providing immediate geographic context.

The per-capita toggle reveals a counter-narrative hidden in absolute totals: while China leads in absolute emissions by 2019, it remains well below the US and Saudi Arabia in per-capita terms. Color encodes country identity consistently across all views. Key historical annotations (USSR collapse, Kyoto Protocol, China's WTO entry, the 2008 financial crisis, the Paris Agreement) provide contextual anchors directly on the chart. Hover tooltips show rank and share of the total, encouraging users to think comparatively rather than in isolation.

Interaction Techniques

Nine interactions are implemented: (1) country toggles with flag icons filter all views simultaneously; (2) a draggable year scrubber synchronizes the bar chart, donut, map, and year display; (3) animated playback auto-advances the scrubber with adjustable speed; (4) a D3 brush on the context chart zooms the main chart's time window; (5) a per-capita / absolute toggle transforms the data across all charts; (6) a chart type toggle switches between line and stacked area modes; (7) hover highlighting dims other lines and shows an enhanced tooltip; (8) event annotation markers are clickable to jump the scrubber to that year; (9) map hover tooltips show country details on the choropleth.

Data Sources

CO2 emissions: World Bank Open Data (EN.ATM.CO2E.KT) — data.worldbank.org. Population: World Bank (SP.POP.TOTL) — data.worldbank.org. World map topology: Natural Earth via world-atlas@2.

Development Process

This project was completed individually. Total development time was approximately 20 hours. The most complex aspects were implementing coordinated linked views across four panels, the D3 brush with animated main chart transitions, the stacked area mode, the choropleth world map with TopoJSON, and the per-capita data transform with smooth axis rescaling. AI was used to aid the research and development process.
