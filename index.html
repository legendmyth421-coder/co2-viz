About This Visualization

Question

Which countries drove global CO2 emissions growth from 1990 to 2019, and how do their trajectories look in both absolute and per-capita terms? This dashboard lets users interactively explore emission trends for five major economies — the United States, China, Germany, India, and Japan — across multiple coordinated views.

Design Rationale

The main view uses a multi-line time-series chart — the most natural encoding for continuous change across entities. A coordinated horizontal bar chart on the right shows ranked emissions for a draggable "scrubber" year, making it easy to see how country rankings shifted decade by decade. A brush overview chart below the main chart enables intuitive temporal zooming without numeric sliders. Lines animate on load using a path-length reveal technique, reinforcing the temporal direction of the data.

The per-capita toggle reveals a counter-narrative hidden in absolute totals: while China leads in absolute emissions by 2019, it remains well below the US in per-capita terms. Color encodes country identity consistently across all views. Key historical annotations (China's WTO entry, the 2008 financial crisis, the Paris Agreement) provide contextual anchors directly on the chart. Hover tooltips show rank and share of the total, encouraging users to think comparatively rather than in isolation.

Interaction Techniques

Seven interactions are implemented: (1) country toggles filter all views simultaneously; (2) a draggable year scrubber synchronizes the bar chart and year display; (3) animated playback auto-advances the scrubber with adjustable speed; (4) a D3 brush on the context chart zooms the main chart's time window; (5) a per-capita / absolute toggle transforms the data and rescales both charts; (6) hover highlighting dims other lines and shows an enhanced tooltip; (7) event annotation markers are clickable to jump the scrubber to that year.

Data Sources

CO2 emissions: World Bank Open Data (EN.ATM.CO2E.KT) — data.worldbank.org. Population: World Bank (SP.POP.TOTL) — data.worldbank.org.

Development Process

This project was completed individually. Total development time was approximately 14 hours. The most complex aspects were implementing coordinated linked views (scrubber → bar chart sync), the D3 brush with animated main chart transitions, and the per-capita data transform with smooth axis rescaling. AI was used to aid the research and development process.
