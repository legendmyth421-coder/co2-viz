About This Visualization

Question

Which countries drove global CO2 emissions growth from 1990 to 2023, and what does the projected trajectory look like to 2030? This dashboard lets users interactively explore emission trends for 11 major economies across multiple coordinated views.

Design Rationale

Seven coordinated chart types provide complementary perspectives on the same dataset: a multi-line time-series chart (with gradient fills and forecast dashes), a stacked area mode, a ranked horizontal bar chart, a donut share chart, a choropleth world map, a country×year heatmap, and a GDP-vs-emissions bubble chart. KPI headline cards surface the most important numbers at a glance. Three metric modes — absolute, per capita, and GDP intensity — let users explore different dimensions. Region grouping aggregates to Americas/Asia/Europe. Compare mode shows side-by-side stat cards for any two countries.

Interaction Techniques

Fifteen interactions: country toggles with flags; draggable year scrubber; animated playback; D3 brush zoom; absolute/per-capita/intensity toggle; line vs stacked area; region grouping; compare mode; hover highlighting; clickable event annotations; map hover; heatmap cell click; bubble hover; country fact cards (double-click); cumulative counter; per-chart SVG export; full-dashboard PNG export.

Data Sources

CO2 emissions: World Bank (EN.ATM.CO2E.KT). Population: World Bank (SP.POP.TOTL). GDP: World Bank (NY.GDP.MKTP.CD). World map: Natural Earth via world-atlas@2. Forecast: linear extrapolation from 2020–2023 trend.

Development Process

This project was completed individually. AI was used to aid the research and development process.
